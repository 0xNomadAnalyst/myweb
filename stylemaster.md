# STYLE SYSTEM — ANALYTICAL DARK (FINANCIAL TERMINAL)

---

## DESIGN INTENT

The website must visually harmonise with professional financial analytics dashboards.

It represents realtime financial infrastructure and risk systems.

The aesthetic should resemble:

- institutional trading platforms

- quantitative research environments

- market analytics systems

NOT startup dark mode or pure black developer themes.

---

## CORE PRINCIPLE

**Dark does not mean black.**

The interface should feel:

- controlled

- engineered

- calm

- low-glare

- long-session readable

Users should feel comfortable viewing the page for extended analytical work.

---

## COLOR FOUNDATION

### ❌ Forbidden

Never use:

#000000  
#050505  
pure black backgrounds

---

### ✅ Primary Background

Use deep navy or graphite tones:

Base background: #0B1220  
Primary surface: #0F172A  
Secondary surface: #111827  
Elevated panels: #172033

These should resemble professional trading interfaces.

---

### Surface Hierarchy

Create visible layering:

Page background  
→ Section container  
→ Card surface  
→ Interactive panel

Depth must come from tone differences, not shadows.

---

## TEXT COLORS

Primary text: #E5E7EB  
Secondary text: #9CA3AF  
Muted text: #6B7280

Avoid stark white text.

Readable ≠ high contrast.

---

## ACCENT COLOR

Accent should harmonise with dashboard blues.

Recommended:

Primary accent: #3B82F6  
Muted accent: #60A5FA

Use sparingly.

Accent indicates interaction or importance only.

---

## DASHBOARD HARMONISATION RULE

Website dark tone should be **slightly lighter** than dashboard dark tone.

Result:

Website → analytical context  
Dashboard → operational depth

The dashboard should visually feel deeper and more powerful.

---

## COMPONENT STYLE

Cards and panels must:

- have subtle borders (`rgba(255,255,255,0.06)`)

- avoid heavy drop shadows

- rely on tonal separation

- maintain consistent radius (6–8px)

Avoid glow or neon edges.

---

## TYPOGRAPHY

Authority comes from restraint.

Use:

- Inter

- Geist

- IBM Plex Sans

Rules:

- medium weights preferred

- tight headline hierarchy

- generous vertical spacing

- avoid oversized hero text

Text should resemble professional system documentation.

---

## MOTION

Allowed:

- subtle fade-in

- minimal hover elevation

- smooth transitions

Forbidden:

- animated gradients

- parallax

- attention-seeking motion

Movement must never compete with data.

---

## VISUAL RELATIONSHIP TO DASHBOARD

The marketing page must feel like:

> the analytical briefing layer sitting above a live financial system.

Not a separate product website.

Transitions from site → dashboard should feel natural.

---

## SUCCESS CRITERION

A sophisticated visitor should subconsciously think:

> This looks like infrastructure used to monitor markets.

NOT:

> This looks like a startup landing page.
