# Opus website summary note for capabilities deck support

## What this site is
Opus presents an independent financial systems analyst practice focused on helping teams understand and operate complex financial systems in real time, especially across digital-asset and onchain markets. The positioning is deliberately principal-led: analytical judgment and technical implementation are combined in one practitioner rather than split across strategy and delivery layers.

## Core positioning
- **Primary promise:** help teams "understand and operate complex financial systems in realtime environments."
- **Practitioner profile:** Roderick McKinley, CFA, FRM.
- **Operating model:** independent practice spanning financial risk analysis, empirical modelling, and realtime infrastructure, from problem definition through deployment.
- **Audience fit:** teams working in complex financial or digital-asset environments that need better visibility, risk understanding, and operational decision support.

## Main navigation / page flow
The homepage is structured as:
1. Hero
2. Operational dashboard / platform section
3. Operational problem domains
4. Analytical approach
5. Ways of working
6. Background & perspective
7. Availability / contact

This means a downloadable capabilities deck should likely mirror that same narrative arc: **problem -> platform -> capabilities -> method -> engagement model -> credibility -> next step**.

## Key messages by section

### 1) Hero / above-the-fold
The hero frames the practice around real-time operation of complex financial systems. It emphasizes:
- principal-led analytical + technical delivery
- digital-asset market specialization
- support from problem definition to deployment
- themes including DeFi risk visibility, protocol/market structure, onchain data, realtime monitoring, and operational intelligence

There are also placeholders for:
- a **3-minute platform overview**
- a **downloadable capabilities overview (PDF)**

This strongly suggests the future deck should act as the site's formal leave-behind asset.

### 2) Operational Intelligence Platform
This section positions the offering as an **operational intelligence environment** rather than a static analytics service. The platform messaging stresses:
- continuous visibility into liquidity, execution quality, and protocol risk
- real-time decision support
- readiness for both **Solana** and **EVM** environments
- an existing analytical framework with protocol mechanics already modelled

The showcased dashboard views imply the productized output is practical and operator-facing, including:
- swap flow / trade count monitoring
- loan distribution / health factor analysis
- OHLCV price and volume analysis
- fixed vs variable rate spread monitoring

### 3) Operational problem domains
The site identifies five recurring problem areas:
- Liquidity & market structure visibility
- Execution quality & price impact
- Protocol & lending risk surveillance
- Stress propagation & contagion
- Operational decision support

This section is especially useful for the capabilities deck because it already defines the clearest **problem-led chapter headings**.

### 4) Analytical approach
The site’s approach is built around three principles:
- **Empirical First** — models grounded in observed behaviour
- **Operationally Oriented** — analysis for decisions, monitoring, and control
- **End-to-End Ownership** — from definition through deployment and handover

It also introduces a critical conceptual point: onchain systems must be understood at **protocol-level resolution**, through protocol mechanics, capital flows, and system dependencies. The included diagram uses **Kamino Lending** to show account architecture across market, reserve, and obligation accounts.

This means the deck should not sound like generic analytics consulting; it should sound like **system-level operational analysis grounded in protocol mechanics**.

### 5) Ways of working
The engagement model is defined through three themes:
- **Principal-Led Delivery** — direct access to the person doing the work
- **Integrated Advisory & Build** — analysis and implementation stay aligned
- **Designed for Internal Ownership** — systems and frameworks are handed over so internal teams can operate and extend them

This suggests the deck should reassure buyers that the work is:
- senior-led
- implementation-capable
- not dependent on a black-box retained service

### 6) Background & perspective
The background section provides the intellectual and commercial rationale for the practice:
- blockchain systems create genuinely new financial structures
- traditional finance and economics provide grounding for interpreting them clearly
- success depends on treating blockchain networks as observable financial systems
- public onchain data is valuable but often only usable with serious analytical/infrastructure work
- the practitioner’s career has centered on data-driven decision support under uncertainty

This section also sharpens the tone of the brand:
- empirically grounded
- skeptical of hype and ideology
- focused on clarity, observability, and operational correctness

### 8) Contact / CTA
The final section frames availability as:

- focused on environments requiring analytical clarity and operational correctness
- typically spanning risk analysis, system design, and implementation

- high-trust
- anti-hype

- inflated "AI platform" language

- overly salesy claims

- precise
- calm
- system-literate
- technically credible
- commercially serious
