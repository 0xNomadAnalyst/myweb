# Professional Context & Writing Style Guide (for agent)

## Context
This content represents a solo independent practitioner (sole trader), not a company or agency.

Role identity:
- Independent Financial Systems Analyst (CFA, FRM)
- Works at the intersection of financial risk analysis, empirical modelling, and operational intelligence infrastructure for digital-asset / DeFi environments
- Core value: collapsing problem definition, analysis/modelling, and implementation into one accountable practitioner (no loss in translation)

Core flagship offering:
- Realtime operational monitoring station for DeFi systems (DEX pricing/liquidity as reference layer, plus lending/yield/exposure monitoring)
Key differentiation:
- Independent market maker / liquidity provider monitoring with measurable KPIs enabling renegotiation, incentive alignment, replacement, or in-house MM decisions
Supporting premium capabilities:
- Cross-protocol risk surfaces and unified monitoring across protocols
- Incident replay / root-cause analysis enabled by historical event store
- Simulation modelling for risk policy design and evaluation, including sizing liquidity provisions and assessing costs of new token mechanisms
Engagement model:
- Principal-led; integrated advisory + build; designed for internal ownership

## Audience
Deck readers are:
- protocol founders / COOs / treasury & risk leads
- trading / market operations teams
- technically literate stakeholders who value rigor and operational outcomes
The deck may be forwarded internally; it must be easy to scan.

## Voice & Tone
- Calm, precise, institutional
- Understated confidence; no hype
- Factual language over persuasion
- Avoid “startup” or “agency” voice

Avoid:
- buzzwords (cutting-edge, world-class, revolutionary)
- inflated promises
- vague “solutions” language
- technical theatre (stack lists, tool name-dropping)

Prefer:
- operational language (visibility, monitoring, exposure, stress, decision support)
- measurable, concrete outcomes (KPIs, evaluation, root-cause analysis)
- clear nouns and verbs; short sentences where possible

## Copy Rules
1) One claim per sentence.
2) Use active voice.
3) Keep bullets decision-oriented: what is enabled, what becomes measurable, what actions become possible.
4) Maintain hierarchy:
   - flagship monitoring first
   - differentiated MM oversight next
   - system-level cross-protocol visibility
   - incident replay and modelling as premium add-ons
5) Do not imply capabilities not actually delivered (e.g., routing optimisation).

## Positioning Guardrails
- Do not present as a “firm” or “team”.
- Use “I” and “my work” where appropriate; avoid “we”.
- Avoid over-indexing on “blockchain” as identity; treat it as the current operating environment.
- Emphasize seriousness: “teams committed to safe operation”, “mission-critical visibility”.

## Output Expectations for Agent
When drafting or editing text:
- Keep slide copy tight and scannable
- No long paragraphs on slides
- Prefer 3–5 bullets per slide
- Keep language consistent with the above tone
